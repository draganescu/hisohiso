pick your weird little machine

Agent cockpit?
Secret tunnel? Tiny room?

There are lots of good ways to poke an agent from a phone. Some are better cockpits. Some are better chat apps. Hisohiso is the tiny encrypted room you use when the private line is the product.

Use with agents See the security model

Paseo is the shiny cockpit.

If you want the whole flight deck for coding agents, Paseo is the obvious place to look. It is built for steering agents across devices, providers, repos, branches, schedules, voice, and GitHub-shaped workflows.

Paseo is better for

Running the agent circus

Provider switching, worktrees, scheduled jobs, native app feel, voice loops, PR flow, and lots of sessions with real orchestration around them.

Hisohiso is better for

Keeping one line private

An accountless room, a link-secret, a ciphertext router, self-hostable plumbing, and phone-native approvals without buying into a full agent workspace.

Happy and mobile agent apps are the nice remotes.

Happy, MobileCLI, Yep Anywhere, AnyCode, 1Code-style tools, and the rest of the phone-agent crowd are chasing a real need: nobody wants to babysit a terminal all day. They can be better when you want a polished app that knows about the agent product first.

  • They are better at app polish. Onboarding, native controls, provider setup, notifications, and repo-focused workflows are where they can shine.
  • Hisohiso is better at disappearing. No account directory, no team graph, no workspace to administer, no permanent social layer around the task.
  • The phone is not the workshop. Hisohiso treats the phone as a steering wheel: tap, approve, inspect, nudge, then let the real machine keep working.

Conductor, Superset, and OpenChamber are the control rooms.

If your problem is coordinating agents, branches, workspaces, terminals, and developer workflows, use a control-room product. That is a bigger game than hisohiso is trying to play.

They are better for

Seeing the whole factory

Agent fleets, project dashboards, workspace state, parallel coding loops, and higher-level developer operations.

Hisohiso is better for

Opening a private hatch

A small encrypted room between a person, a phone, and a local process — with just enough UI for diffs, commands, buttons, and danger confirmations.

Signal, Telegram, Slack, and Matrix are real chat.

Use them for everyday groups, teams, contacts, search, history, social context, and all the normal human mess. Hisohiso is for the opposite mood: make a room, use the room, burn the room down when it is no longer useful.

  • Chat apps are better for people you know. Contacts, workspaces, notifications, identity, files, search, and long-running group life.
  • Hisohiso is better for people you do not want indexed. No phone number, no profile, no contact graph, no public lobby, no readable server-side message history.
  • Agent output should not be a wall of chat. A diff, command log, button row, checklist, or long-press approval deserves a real phone surface.

SSH, tmux, Tailscale, and tunnels are the wrench drawer.

They are powerful, boring, and excellent. If you want raw access to a machine, use them. Hisohiso is not trying to beat a shell at being a shell; it gives agents and humans a safer little room for requests, answers, and approvals.

The vibe check.

Want a cockpit? Pick a cockpit. Want a remote shell? Open a shell. Want a tiny private room where your phone, your agent, and maybe another human can whisper without an account? That is hisohiso.
