the honest comparison

We're not for everything.
Here's where we fit.

There are a lot of lovely ways to talk to an agent from your phone, and most of them beat us at something. So here's the honest version — what each one is wonderful at, and the single mood where a tiny encrypted room is the right call.

Use with agents See the security model

Paseo gives you the whole cockpit.

When you want the full flight deck — every provider, every repo, branches, schedules, voice, the whole GitHub dance — Paseo is genuinely good at it. We're not trying to be that. Honestly, we'd reach for it too when the job is flying a lot of agents at once.

Reach for Paseo when

You're running the whole show

Switching providers, juggling worktrees, scheduled jobs, voice loops, PR flow — a real cockpit with real instruments, in an app that earns its polish.

Reach for us when

You just want one quiet line

No account, a secret that lives in the link, a server that only ever sees ciphertext, and plumbing you can host yourself. One room — not a workspace.

Happy and friends sweat the details.

Happy, MobileCLI, Yep Anywhere, AnyCode, and the rest of the phone-agent crowd are chasing something real: nobody wants to babysit a terminal all day. When you want a beautiful app that thinks about the agent first, they'll often feel nicer than we do — and that's fine.

  • They make a lovely app. Onboarding, native controls, provider setup, notifications — the things a good product is supposed to get right.
  • We'd rather disappear. No account directory, no team graph, no workspace to keep tidy, no social layer lingering around your task.
  • The phone is a steering wheel, not a workshop. Tap, approve, glance, nudge — then let the real machine keep working.

Herding a fleet? That's a control room's job.

When the real problem is coordinating agents, branches, terminals, and workflows, Conductor, Superset, and OpenChamber are built for exactly that. It's a bigger, busier game than the one we're playing — and we're happy to leave it to them.

Reach for them when

You can see the whole factory

Agent fleets, project dashboards, workspace state, parallel coding loops, and the higher-level view of everything in flight.

Reach for us when

You just need a private hatch

A small encrypted room between a person, a phone, and one local process — with just enough UI for diffs, commands, buttons, and a careful yes.

For real conversations, use real chat.

Signal, Telegram, Slack, Matrix — keep those for the people in your life: groups, history, search, the warm everyday mess. We're built for the opposite mood. Open a room, use it, and close it the moment it stops being useful.

  • Chat apps are for people you know. Contacts, notifications, identity, files, search, the long life of a group thread.
  • We're for the things you'd rather not see indexed. No phone number, no profile, no contact graph, no public lobby, nothing readable left on the server.
  • An agent's answer isn't a chat bubble. A diff, a command log, a row of buttons, a long-press approval — those deserve a real surface, not a wall of text.

Need raw access to a box? Open the wrench drawer.

SSH, tmux, Tailscale, a quiet little tunnel — powerful, boring, and wonderful. If you want a shell, use a shell; we're not going to out-shell the shell. We're after something gentler: a small room where a person and an agent can ask, answer, and agree, with nobody setting up an account.

So, which one's your mood?

Want a cockpit? Go fly it. Want a shell? Open it. But if what you really want is a small, private room where your phone, your agent, and maybe one trusted person can whisper — no account, no audience, gone when you're done — that's the one we made.
